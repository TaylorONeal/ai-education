# Brand Guidelines

A reusable design document for the course-page-generator skill. Fill this in once (or have the skill capture it for you), save it, and the generator reads it so every page matches your brand. Keep it in your prof-brain under a brand note if you use that skill.

## Colors

| Role | Hex | Notes |
|------|-----|-------|
| Background | #______ | The page background. Dark or light. |
| Body text | #______ | Must pass contrast against the background (aim for 4.5:1 or better). |
| Primary accent | #______ | Headers, primary section borders. |
| Secondary accent | #______ | Alternating sections. |
| Highlight accent | #______ | Tips, callouts, calls to action. |
| Link | #______ | High contrast on the background, underlined. |

Contrast check: confirm body text and each accent are readable on the background before using. Do not rely on color alone to carry meaning; always pair it with a text label.

## Type

- Font stack: ______ (use a web-safe stack with a system fallback; do not embed custom fonts, the LMS editor strips them).
- Heading weight and sizes: H1 ~32px, H2 ~22px, body ~16px, line height ~1.7 (adjust to taste).

## Accent cycling

The three accents cycle across section blocks (primary, secondary, highlight, then repeat). Optionally fix an accent by meaning, for example: overview and grading always primary, submission and resources always secondary, tips and examples always highlight.
