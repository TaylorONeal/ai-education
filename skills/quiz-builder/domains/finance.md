# Domain Pack: Finance

Scenarios for a finance course. The student values a cash flow, judges a project, or reasons about risk and return.

## Scenario entities and decisions

- A firm deciding whether to take a project using discounted cash flows.
- An investor comparing assets on risk and return, or building a diversified portfolio.
- A valuation that turns on the time value of money and the right discount rate.
- A capital-structure or cost-of-capital judgment.

## Metrics, frameworks, and vocabulary (with realistic ranges)

- Time value: present and future value, discounting, net present value, internal rate of return, payback period, the discount rate.
- Risk and return: the risk-return tradeoff, diversification, systematic (market) versus unsystematic (firm-specific) risk, beta, the capital asset pricing model, the weighted average cost of capital.
- Vocabulary: free cash flow, terminal value, bond yield and price (inverse relationship), duration, the role of debt in the capital structure, dividends, sunk cost, market efficiency. Typical discount rates often fall in the 6% to 14% range.

## What good distractors look like here

- Ignoring the time value of money: treating a dollar next year as a dollar today.
- Including a sunk cost in a capital-budgeting decision, or omitting opportunity cost.
- Confusing IRR and NPV decision rules, or trusting IRR when cash flows are unconventional.
- Believing diversification removes all risk, rather than removing only the unsystematic part.
- Expecting higher return with no added risk, or confusing total volatility with systematic risk.

## House style: two worked examples

Scenario, hard: "A project needs $100K now and returns $60K in year one and $60K in year two. At a 10% discount rate, should the firm accept it, and what rule decides?" Correct answer: discount the cash flows (about $54.5K plus $49.6K, near $104K) for a positive NPV of roughly $4K, so accept; NPV is the decision rule because it measures value created in today's dollars. Distractors: reject because the simple sum logic is wrong, accept only on payback, say IRR and NPV must always agree.

Scenario, medium: "An investor already holds 30 stocks across many industries and adds one more. Which kind of risk does the new stock mostly help with, and what is the limit?" Correct answer: adding it reduces unsystematic, firm-specific risk through diversification, but systematic market risk remains and cannot be diversified away. Distractors: it removes all risk, it removes market risk, diversification does nothing at this point.

## Feedback voice

Correct-answer feedback names the principle (time value, the decision rule, the kind of risk). Incorrect-answer feedback names the error, for example a sunk-cost inclusion or confusing systematic with unsystematic risk, without giving away the answer.
