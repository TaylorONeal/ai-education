# Domain Pack: Marketing

Scenarios for a marketing course. The student is the marketer deciding what to measure, how to position, or which channel and message fit a goal.

## Scenario entities and decisions

- A brand choosing a positioning, a target segment, or a channel mix.
- A campaign across paid, owned, and earned channels that has to be judged and adjusted.
- A product launch using the marketing mix (product, price, place, promotion).
- A customer journey from awareness to loyalty, with a drop-off somewhere to diagnose.

## Metrics, frameworks, and vocabulary (with realistic ranges)

- Funnel and frameworks: the marketing funnel (awareness, consideration, conversion, loyalty), AIDA, STP (segmentation, targeting, positioning), the 4Ps, customer journey mapping, brand equity, positioning statement.
- Metrics: customer acquisition cost ($10 to $300 by channel), lifetime value, the LTV-to-CAC ratio (healthy near 3:1), conversion rate (1% to 5%), click-through rate (0.5% to 5%), return on ad spend (1x to 5x), average order value, market share, net promoter score (negative to +70), brand awareness (aided and unaided), retention and churn.

## What good distractors look like here

- Confusing a vanity metric (impressions, follower count) with a performance metric (conversion, CAC).
- Treating awareness as if it were conversion, or reach as if it were engagement.
- Confusing positioning (the place you own in the customer's mind) with promotion (how you advertise).
- Attributing a sales lift to a campaign without ruling out seasonality or other channels (correlation as causation in attribution).
- Picking the segment that is largest rather than the one that fits the value proposition.

## House style: two worked examples

Scenario, medium: "A brand's awareness campaign drove a 40% lift in impressions and no change in sales. The team calls it a success. What is the strongest critique?" Correct answer: impressions are an upper-funnel vanity signal; with no movement in conversion or revenue, the campaign has not been shown to create value, and the right next step is to measure mid-funnel consideration or conversion. Distractors: praise the reach (the error being tested), blame the budget (unsupported), conclude the product is bad (overreach).

Concept, medium: "A startup describes its strategy as 'we will advertise on more channels.' Why is this positioning advice incomplete?" Correct answer: that is promotion, a tactic, not positioning; positioning defines the distinct place the brand holds for a specific segment relative to alternatives. Distractors: confuse it with pricing, with product features, or call it fine as stated.

## Feedback voice

Correct-answer feedback names the principle (the funnel stage, the framework, the metric's purpose). Incorrect-answer feedback names the confusion the option represents, for example mistaking a vanity metric for a performance metric, without restating the right answer verbatim.
