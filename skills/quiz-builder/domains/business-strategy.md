# Domain Pack: Business Strategy

Scenarios for a strategy course. The student is the analyst or executive judging an industry, a competitive position, or a strategic choice.

## Scenario entities and decisions

- A firm choosing among generic strategies (cost leadership, differentiation, focus).
- An industry analyzed for attractiveness and the forces shaping its profitability.
- A company deciding whether a resource is a real source of advantage or just a strength.
- A diversification, entry, or exit decision in a specific competitive context.

## Metrics, frameworks, and vocabulary (with realistic ranges)

- Frameworks: Porter's Five Forces, the value chain, generic strategies, SWOT, PESTEL, the resource-based view (VRIO: valuable, rare, inimitable, organized), core competency, the BCG growth-share matrix, blue ocean versus red ocean, first-mover advantage, switching costs.
- Vocabulary: competitive advantage, sustainable advantage, industry structure, barriers to entry, bargaining power, complements, vertical and horizontal integration, economies of scale and scope.

## What good distractors look like here

- Confusing a strength (SWOT) with a competitive advantage (VRIO): a capability rivals can copy is not an advantage.
- Confusing tactics with strategy, or activities with positioning.
- Mistaking industry attractiveness for a single firm's performance, or vice versa.
- Treating a SWOT laundry list as analysis without prioritizing or connecting items to action.
- Assigning a force to the wrong category (calling a substitute a new entrant, or supplier power buyer power).

## House style: two worked examples

Scenario, hard: "A grocery chain has the lowest prices in its region because of scale-driven purchasing. Three rivals are building similar scale. Using VRIO, is this a sustainable advantage?" Correct answer: the cost position is valuable and currently rare, but if rivals can match the scale, it is imitable, so it is an advantage now but not a sustainable one. Distractors: call it sustainable (ignores imitability), call it irrelevant (ignores current value), confuse it with differentiation.

Concept, medium: "New entrants face high capital costs and incumbents control distribution. Which of Porter's forces is this, and what does it imply?" Correct answer: high barriers to entry mean the threat of new entrants is low, which supports incumbent profitability. Distractors: label it buyer power, supplier power, or rivalry.

## Feedback voice

Correct-answer feedback names the framework and the specific criterion (which VRIO test fails, which force applies). Incorrect-answer feedback names the mix-up, for example strength versus advantage or tactic versus strategy, without giving away the answer.
