# Company Catalog

A reusable cast of real companies for scenario questions, so examples feel current and relevant instead of generic placeholders. Pick a set that fits the course and the energy you want, then tell the generator to draw scenarios from it. This pairs with any domain pack: the domain pack sets the field, the catalog sets the cast.

Companies named here are public and used only as illustrative scenario subjects. Keep scenario numbers invented and plausible; do not present made-up figures as a real company's actual results.

## Choosing a set

- Modern digital and direct-to-consumer: relatable and exciting to most students, strong for funnels, growth, social, subscription, and app scenarios.
- Traditional and established: best for operations, supply chain, manufacturing, and for comparison questions about how an incumbent differs from a digital challenger.
- Big tech and platforms: scale, network effects, platform economics, two-sided markets.
- Mixed: the most useful for discriminating questions, because contrasting a digital-native and a legacy firm in the same scenario forces real analysis rather than pattern-matching.

## Modern digital and direct-to-consumer

Glossier, Gymshark, Allbirds, Warby Parker, Bombas, Dollar Shave Club, Casper, Chewy, Peloton, Liquid Death, Olipop, Athletic Greens. Good for: acquisition and retention, influencer and social campaigns, subscription and churn, brand building, conversion funnels.

## Big tech and platforms

Apple, Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Meta, Netflix, Spotify, Uber, Airbnb, DoorDash, Nvidia, Salesforce, Adobe, Figma, Duolingo. Good for: platform and network-effect scenarios, two-sided marketplaces, freemium and subscription economics, scale and infrastructure, product analytics.

## Traditional and established

Coca-Cola, Procter and Gamble, General Motors, Ford, Walmart, McDonald's, FedEx, UPS, Boeing, Caterpillar, John Deere, 3M, Kellogg's, Disney, Target, Kroger. Good for: operations and supply chain, manufacturing and logistics, mature-market competition, distribution, brand portfolios, incumbent-versus-challenger contrasts.

## Retail and consumer

Costco, Sephora, Lululemon, Trader Joe's, Nike, Starbucks, IKEA, Best Buy. Good for: retail operations, inventory and merchandising, loyalty, omnichannel, customer experience.

## Finance and fintech

JPMorgan Chase, Visa, PayPal, Block, Robinhood, Coinbase, Chime, Stripe. Good for: finance, payments, fintech disruption, risk, unit economics, regulation-aware scenarios.

## How to use it in the prompt

Add a line to the quiz-builder prompt: "Draw scenario companies from the [SET] in companies.md. Keep all numbers invented and plausible; do not state real financial results." For a contrast question, name one company from two sets and ask the student to reason about the difference. Refresh the lists over time so the examples stay current and the questions keep feeling alive to students.
